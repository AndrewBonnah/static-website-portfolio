AI vs Human Text: Quick Analysis

Last time I generated 20,000 GPT-2 text sequences and compared them to other AI and human-written corpora, making this image:

Image from previous analysis, placing our dataset amongst other known AI and Human datasets.

Next, we're taking a closer look at the details of AI vs human text using token-level divergence measures. Instead of global stats like lexical diversity or sentence length, these metrics check differences in individual word choices, sequences, and phrasing.

Here are the main measures I'll be gathering:

  • KL Divergence (Kullback-Leibler): How surprising AI text looks if you assume it was written like human text. "If I think this text is human, how wrong am I?"
  • Jensen-Shannon Divergence (JSD): A symmetric, more stable version of KL. Always finite, easier to interpret, and common in NLP research. "How far apart are human and AI word-choice patterns?"
  • Perplexity Gap: Measures how "strange" AI text looks to a language model trained on human text. "Does this text look easy or unusual to a human-trained model?"
  • N-gram Divergence: Looks at sequences of words (bigrams, trigrams) to see patterns in phrasing. AI often repeats certain patterns, while humans vary more naturally.
  • Burstiness / Repetition Metrics: Measures token reuse, phrase recurrence, and long-range repetition. "Does the text get stuck saying the same thing in slightly different ways?"

Where global measures failed, token-level divergence may reveal the subtle stylistic differences that make AI and human text distinct.
